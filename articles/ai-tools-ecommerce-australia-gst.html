AI Tools for Australian eCommerce Businesses: GST, Shipping, and Customer Service

March 23, 2026 · 8 min read

Running an online store in Australia comes with a specific set of challenges that most global eCommerce platforms are not fully designed for. GST applies to most goods and services, but the rules for digital goods, imported items, and cross-border sales are complicated. Shipping within Australia is expensive compared to many other markets. And customer service expectations are high.

AI tools are increasingly useful for solving each of these problems. Here is where they make the most difference for Australian online stores.

GST Compliance for Online Sales

If your Australian online business has a GST turnover of $75,000 or more, you must be registered for GST and include it in your prices. For most consumer goods sold to Australian customers, this means your prices are effectively 10% GST-inclusive. You need to display and remit this correctly.

The complexity increases if you sell internationally. Goods exported to overseas customers are GST-free. Digital services sold to Australian consumers by overseas businesses are subject to GST under the "Netflix tax" rules that came into effect in 2017. And if you sell low-value goods imported from overseas (under $1,000), GST is collected at the point of sale.

AI-powered platforms like Shopify handle most of this automatically once configured correctly for the Australian market. The tax engine in Shopify applies the right GST rate to Australian customers, zero-rates exports, and generates the transaction records needed for your BAS. Connecting Shopify to an accounting tool like Xero via an integration like A2X automates the bookkeeping side, so your sales figures feed directly into your accounting records without manual data entry.

Shipping Optimisation

Shipping is one of the biggest pain points for Australian eCommerce businesses. Australia Post rates have increased significantly, and competing carriers have variable coverage outside major cities. AI tools can help optimise your shipping decisions in a few ways.

Platforms like Shippit use AI to compare rates across multiple carriers for each order and recommend the best option based on price, delivery time, and destination. This can meaningfully reduce your average shipping cost, particularly for businesses sending a mix of small parcels and larger items to addresses across Australia, including rural areas where carrier options vary significantly.

AI inventory management tools like Dear Commerce or Cin7 can forecast demand and flag when stock levels are likely to create fulfilment delays, which reduces the customer service burden of dealing with backorder enquiries.

AI Customer Service for Online Stores

Customer service is one of the biggest time sinks for small eCommerce businesses. Most enquiries are variations of the same handful of questions: where is my order, can I return this, what size should I buy, and do you ship to my location. AI chatbots can handle all of these without human involvement.

Gorgias is the leading customer service platform for Shopify stores and has strong AI features. It can automatically respond to order status enquiries by pulling data from your Shopify store, handle return requests according to your policy, and route complex issues to a human. For stores getting more than ten customer enquiries per day, Gorgias typically reduces the human time spent on customer service by 40 to 60%.

Tidio and Zendesk are alternatives that work across more platforms including WooCommerce and Squarespace. Both have AI-powered auto-response features that improve over time as they learn which answers resolve customer issues.

AI Product Descriptions and SEO

Writing product descriptions for a large catalogue is one of the most time-consuming content tasks in eCommerce. AI tools can generate product descriptions in bulk from your specifications. Shopify Magic, Shopify's built-in AI writing feature, can draft descriptions directly from the product page. Claude and ChatGPT can do the same for other platforms when you feed them the product details.

The descriptions benefit from a final human review, particularly for accuracy and to ensure they reflect your brand voice, but they provide a solid starting point that typically requires only minor editing.

Returns and Refunds Under Australian Consumer Law

Australian Consumer Law gives customers stronger rights than many other markets. Customers are entitled to a refund, replacement, or repair for products that are faulty, not fit for purpose, or not as described, and these rights exist regardless of your posted returns policy. AI customer service tools need to be configured to handle returns according to both your policy and ACL requirements.

The practical implication is that if you are using an AI chatbot to handle return requests, it should be set up to always offer an ACL remedy for clearly defective products rather than defaulting to a "no returns" policy that might be legally unenforceable.

Starting an online store?

Set up your GST tax configuration and connect your accounting integration before you make your first sale. Retroactively reconciling months of sales for BAS purposes is far more painful than getting it right from the start.
