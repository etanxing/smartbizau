Using AI for Cash Flow Forecasting in Your Australian Small Business

March 23, 2026 · 7 min read

More Australian small businesses fail from cash flow problems than from any other cause. It is not always about making poor business decisions. Sometimes it is about a perfectly healthy business having three large invoices outstanding at the same time as a quarterly BAS payment and a supplier invoice comes due. Without visibility of what is coming, it is impossible to plan.

AI cash flow forecasting tools solve this by connecting to your accounting software, analysing your historical patterns, and projecting your bank balance forward over weeks or months. The best tools flag cash shortfalls before they happen, giving you time to chase invoices, arrange a business overdraft, or defer a non-essential purchase.

Why Cash Flow Forecasting Matters More Than Profit

Profit is what your accountant reports at the end of the year. Cash flow is what keeps your business running week to week. A business can be genuinely profitable while simultaneously running out of cash, particularly if it invoices on 30-day terms, pays suppliers upfront, or has lumpy income patterns.

For Australian small businesses with seasonal revenue, project-based income, or clients who pay slowly, cash flow visibility is critical. Knowing that you are going to have a cash shortfall in six weeks gives you time to do something about it. Finding out when your bank account hits zero does not.

Xero Analytics Plus

Xero's Analytics Plus feature, which comes with their higher-tier plans, includes an AI-powered short-term cash flow forecast. It looks at your outstanding invoices, scheduled bill payments, and historical patterns to project your bank balance over the coming 30 days. The forecast is visual and updates automatically as your data changes.

The 30-day view is useful for immediate planning. The limitation is that it does not project further than 30 days, which is not enough runway for businesses with longer payment cycles or seasonal cash needs.

Float

Float is a dedicated cash flow forecasting tool that integrates with Xero, MYOB, and QuickBooks. It creates scenarios showing your projected bank balance under different assumptions, such as if your largest client pays on time versus 30 days late. The AI uses your historical data to predict when invoices are likely to be paid based on each client's past payment behaviour.

This invoice payment prediction is particularly useful. If you know that a specific client typically pays 15 days late despite 30-day terms, Float factors this into the forecast automatically. The result is a more accurate picture of your real cash position rather than an optimistic assumption that everyone pays on time.

Float pricing starts at around $49 AUD per month, which is significant for a small business but is usually justified if it prevents even one cash crisis per year.

Futrli

Futrli is a more advanced forecasting platform that allows you to build multiple scenarios and compare them. You can model what happens to your cash position if you win a new contract, hire a staff member, or take out a business loan. The AI-powered predictions update as your actual data comes in, and the variance analysis shows you where your projections were wrong and why.

Futrli is better suited to businesses that want to do genuine financial planning rather than just knowing their current trajectory. It is also commonly used by accountants and bookkeepers as part of their advisory services, so your accounting firm may already have access.

Using Your Accounting Software's Native Reporting

If budget is tight, your existing accounting software likely has some cash flow visibility built in. Xero, MYOB, and Rounded all show your outstanding invoices and upcoming bill payments. While this is not a forecast in the traditional sense, building a simple spreadsheet from these numbers is often sufficient for very small businesses.

The limitation of manual spreadsheets is that they go out of date quickly and are only as accurate as the data you put into them. An automated tool connected to your live accounting data is more reliable and requires much less maintenance.

Chasing Invoices with AI

The most direct way to improve cash flow is to get paid faster. AI-powered invoice tools like Rounded and Xero can send automated payment reminders at intervals you choose. A polite reminder sent the day before a due date, and another three days after, significantly improves average payment times without requiring you to make awkward phone calls.

For businesses with persistent late payers, some tools can flag which clients consistently pay late so you can adjust your credit terms or require upfront payments from those clients going forward.

Feeling uncertain about your cash position?

The quickest way to get clarity is to list your outstanding invoices with their due dates, your scheduled bill payments for the next 60 days, and your average monthly fixed costs. Even a rough spreadsheet version of this gives you far more control than flying blind.
