5 AI Invoice Generators That Handle Australian GST and ABN Automatically

March 23, 2026 · 6 min read

Creating invoices should take 30 seconds, not 10 minutes. But if you are an Australian freelancer or sole trader, you know the drill: open a template, fill in the client details, double-check the GST calculation, make sure your ABN is on there, manually number the invoice, and hope you remembered to update the date. Then wait three weeks for payment because you forgot to set up a reminder.

AI invoice generators fix most of this. They remember your clients, auto-calculate GST, include your ABN by default, and chase up late payments without you lifting a finger. Here are five that work well for Australian businesses.

1. Rounded

Rounded was built from the ground up for Australian freelancers. When you create your account and enter your ABN, every invoice you generate from that point on includes it automatically. GST is calculated based on whether you are registered, and the tool knows the difference between GST-free and taxable supplies.

The AI component kicks in with smart client suggestions. Start typing a client name and it auto-fills their details from previous invoices. It also tracks payment status and sends automatic reminders at intervals you set. The free plan covers up to 5 clients, which is enough to test it out.

What makes Rounded stand out is that it feeds directly into your BAS reporting. Every invoice you create is already categorised correctly, so when BAS time comes around, the numbers are ready.

2. Xero Invoicing

Xero is overkill if you only need invoicing, but if you are already using it for accounting, the invoicing module is excellent. The AI learns your invoicing patterns and can suggest line items based on what you have billed a specific client before.

Recurring invoices are fully automated. Set up a template for a retainer client, and Xero sends the invoice, applies GST, and logs the expected income automatically. Payment reminders go out on schedule without any intervention.

The integration with Australian banks means you can match incoming payments to invoices automatically. When a client pays, Xero reconciles it and updates your records. For high-volume invoicing (more than 20 invoices a month), this automation saves hours.

3. Zoho Invoice

Zoho Invoice has a genuinely useful free tier for Australian users. It supports ABN, handles GST calculations, and lets you send up to 1,000 invoices per year at no cost. The AI features include smart categorisation of line items, automatic currency conversion for international clients, and payment reminders.

The standout feature for Australian users is the multi-currency support with automatic AUD conversion. If you do any work for overseas clients, Zoho pulls the current exchange rate and shows both the foreign amount and the AUD equivalent on the invoice. This saves you from manually checking rates every time.

The downside is that Zoho's ecosystem is vast and can feel overwhelming. If you only want invoicing, you need to ignore a lot of features that are trying to upsell you into their CRM, project management, and other tools.

4. Invoice Ninja

Invoice Ninja is open-source and has a free tier that covers most sole trader needs. The AI auto-fill feature learns from your invoicing history and pre-populates new invoices based on the client you select. It handles GST and supports Australian tax invoice formatting requirements.

For the technically inclined, you can self-host Invoice Ninja on your own server for free, which means zero ongoing costs. The hosted version has a free plan for up to 20 clients. The paid plan at $10 USD per month unlocks custom branding, more automation rules, and priority support.

Payment gateway integration is strong. Stripe, PayPal, and direct bank transfer are all supported, and you can offer multiple payment options on a single invoice. Clients click a button and pay immediately, which significantly reduces payment times.

5. Hnry

Hnry deserves a mention here even though it is much more than an invoice generator. When you create an invoice through Hnry, the system automatically calculates how much tax, GST, and super to set aside when the payment comes in. It goes beyond just generating the invoice; it acts on the payment.

For sole traders who want to completely automate the invoicing-to-tax pipeline, Hnry is the closest thing to having a virtual bookkeeper. The trade-off is the 1% fee on income, but for many people the peace of mind is worth it.

The Bottom Line

If you are just starting out and want zero cost, Zoho Invoice or Invoice Ninja's free tiers will cover you. If you are an established Australian freelancer, Rounded is purpose-built for your workflow. And if you want to forget invoicing exists as a task entirely, Hnry handles everything from the invoice to the tax return.

Whichever you choose, the key is to stop spending time on invoices. Let the AI handle the formatting, the GST math, and the awkward payment reminders. Your job is to do the work that generates the invoices in the first place.
