5 AI Invoice Generators That Handle Australian GST and ABN Automatically

March 23, 2026 · 6 min read

Creating invoices sounds simple until you factor in Australian requirements. A proper tax invoice needs your ABN, the words "Tax Invoice" in the header, GST calculated correctly, and the right date format. If you are registered for GST and invoicing over $82.50, getting this wrong can cause real headaches for your clients and for you at BAS time.

The good news is that AI invoice generators have come a long way. The best ones can draft an invoice in seconds, auto-populate your ABN, split out the GST component, and even follow up late payers. Here are five worth looking at.

Rounded

Rounded is built in Australia for Australian freelancers and sole traders, which means it does not need to be told what GST is or how an ABN works. You enter your ABN once during setup, and every invoice after that includes it automatically. The GST is calculated and broken out as a separate line item without any configuration needed.

Where Rounded is particularly useful is in its quote-to-invoice conversion. You draft a quote for a client, they approve it, and Rounded converts it to a tax invoice with one click. Payment reminders go out automatically at the intervals you choose. For a sole trader billing multiple clients, this alone saves a meaningful amount of time each month.

Pricing starts at $13.50 AUD per month.

Invoice Ninja with AI Fill

Invoice Ninja is a free and open-source invoicing platform that added AI-powered auto-fill features in recent years. You can tell it your business details once, and it fills in the correct fields on every invoice. It supports Australian GST natively and can generate invoices in AUD with proper tax invoice formatting.

The free tier is genuinely useful for low-volume invoicing. If you send fewer than 20 invoices a month and do not need automated reminders, the free plan covers you. The paid plans start at around $12 AUD per month and add automated workflows.

Xero with Smart Invoice Templates

If you are already using Xero for accounting, its invoicing module is excellent. The AI-assisted templates remember your common line items, suggest descriptions based on past invoices, and flag anything that looks off compared to your usual billing. ABN and GST handling is seamless because Xero is built for the Australian market.

The one catch is that Xero invoicing requires a Xero subscription, which starts at $29 AUD per month for the Starter plan. If you are only invoicing a handful of clients and do not need full accounting, it is more expensive than dedicated invoice tools. But if you want invoicing and accounting in one place, Xero is worth it.

Zoho Invoice

Zoho Invoice is free for small businesses with fewer than five clients. It handles Australian GST correctly, supports AUD billing, and includes ABN fields in the invoice template. The AI features are more limited than some competitors, but the automation for payment reminders and recurring invoices is solid.

For freelancers just starting out who want a professional invoicing tool without paying monthly fees, Zoho Invoice is one of the better free options available.

FreshBooks

FreshBooks is popular with Australian service businesses and consultants. Their AI-powered invoicing can draft invoices from time tracking entries, suggest line items based on your history, and estimate project costs. GST and ABN support is built in for Australian accounts.

Pricing starts at around $19 AUD per month. The time-tracking-to-invoice workflow is the standout feature if you bill by the hour.

What to Look for in an AI Invoice Generator

When comparing tools for the Australian market, the key requirements are ABN field support, native GST calculation, AUD as the default currency, and proper "Tax Invoice" formatting. Any tool that makes you manually add these things or configure them every time is not worth the trouble.

Payment reminders and automated follow-up are where AI adds the most practical value. Getting paid on time is one of the biggest challenges for sole traders. A tool that sends a polite follow-up email seven days after the due date, and another at fourteen days, removes that awkward conversation from your plate entirely.
